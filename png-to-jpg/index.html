Why convert PNG to JPG?

PNG is lossless and can be large for photographs. JPG uses efficient lossy compression, which often shrinks photo file size dramatically — useful for email attachments, CMS uploads, and faster page loads when transparency isn’t required.

Frequently asked questions

What happens to transparent PNG backgrounds?

JPG has no alpha channel, so transparency is replaced with a solid background (typically white) when the image is drawn to the canvas.

Should I use PNG or JPG for screenshots?

Screenshots with text and flat colors usually look sharper as PNG. JPG is better when you need a smaller file and can tolerate slight compression artifacts.

Can I convert PNG to JPG offline?

After the page loads once, conversion works without uploading files. You can disconnect from the internet and still convert locally.
