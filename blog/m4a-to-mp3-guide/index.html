How to Convert M4A to MP3 (iPhone Voice Memos & Apple Music)

Published: June 20, 2025 · 4 min read

M4A is Apple's default audio format for voice memos, GarageBand projects, and Apple Music downloads. While it offers excellent quality at small file sizes, M4A isn't universally supported. Older car stereos, some Android apps, and certain editing programs won't play or import M4A files. Converting to MP3 solves these compatibility issues instantly.

What is M4A?

M4A is a container format that typically uses the AAC (Advanced Audio Codec) codec. It delivers better audio quality than MP3 at the same bitrate because AAC uses more advanced compression algorithms. However, M4A files are mainly associated with the Apple ecosystem — they play natively on iPhones, iPads, and Macs, but support is inconsistent elsewhere.

Why convert M4A to MP3?

  1. Older devices and car stereos that only support MP3
  2. Android apps that don't recognize M4A files
  3. Audio editing software that requires MP3 or WAV input
  4. Sharing files with people outside the Apple ecosystem
  5. Reducing file size when quality differences are inaudible

Common M4A sources

  1. iPhone Voice Memos app — recordings are saved as M4A by default
  2. GarageBand exports — projects export to M4A (AAC) format
  3. Apple Music downloads — offline tracks use M4A container
  4. Logic Pro exports — professional audio projects can export as M4A
